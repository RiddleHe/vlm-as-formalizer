**Analysis of the Error**

The error "Search stopped without finding a solution" in a PDDL planning context means the planner *can't* find a sequence of actions from the initial state to the goal state, *given the domain and problem definition*. This doesn't necessarily mean there's a PDDL syntax error. It means the problem is likely unsolvable with the actions defined, or the initial and goal states are too far apart to be reached.

Looking at the previous PDDL, the initial state describes three separate stacks:

*   purple on red
*   green on the table
*   yellow on orange on blue

The goal state is a single stack:

*   green on purple on orange on yellow on blue on red

The problem is that the initial state has green on the table, and the goal requires green to be on top of the entire stack.  The actions defined (pick-up, put-down, stack, unstack) don't allow for directly moving a block from the table onto the top of another stack.  The robot needs to pick up green, then stack it on top of the other blocks.

```pddl

    (:domain blocksworld)
    (:objects
        purple - block
        red - block

        (clear green)
        (clear orange)
        (clear blue)
    )
    (:goal (and
        (on green purple)
        (on purple orange)
        (on orange yellow)
        (on yellow blue)
        (on blue red)
    ))
)
```
