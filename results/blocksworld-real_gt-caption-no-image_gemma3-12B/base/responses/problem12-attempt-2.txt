**Analysis of the Error**

The error "Search stopped without finding a solution" in a PDDL planning context means the planner *couldn't* find a sequence of actions to achieve the goal state from the initial state. This doesn't necessarily mean there's a *syntactic* error in the PDDL itself (like a typo in a predicate or action name). It means the problem is either:

1.  **The initial state and goal are incompatible:** The goal is impossible to achieve given the starting configuration.
2.  **The problem is too complex for the planner to solve within a reasonable time:** The planner might be getting stuck in a loop or exploring a very large search space.
3.  **The problem description is flawed:** The initial state or goal doesn't accurately reflect the image/instruction.

The goal is to create three separate stacks: yellow on orange, purple on green, and red on blue.  The initial state *is* incompatible with the goal.
