The error in the previous PDDL problem is due to the mismatch between objects and their types and locations. Specifically:

- The objects carrot_veg and cucumber_veg as vegetables were not held or sliced in the initial state, and also they were assigned to locations (table, chopping_board), but the robot is not mentioned or located, nor is the knife or tool held.
- The knife and robot are not related properly for the slice precondition.
- There are no holding predicates for the robot, so slicing cannot be performed.
- Also, the cucumber was not defined as a vegetable explicitly. We only had cucumber_veg and carrot_veg but no proper matching objects.
- The bowl is at the table but not relevantly connected for placing objects inside.

In summary, the problem is that the initial state doesn't allow the robot to perform the necessary pick actions to hold the carrot and knife prior to slicing or placing.

Looking at the image carefully:
- The carrot is placed on a black tray at front right side.
- The cucumber (cut in half) is on a wooden plate on an elevated platform.
- There is a large kitchen knife held by a robotic arm.
- A white bowl is on left front side on the tray.
- There is also a chopping board on the elevated platform.
- No robot location is explicitly stated, but we must assume the robot can interact with the objects in their locations.

Hence, the problem should include the robot's location, the correct location of objects, and the robot must pick or hold required tools and vegetables to slice and then place into the bowl.

I will define the following based on the image:
- robot1 at tray (or a generic location 'tray')
- carrot and cucumber vegetables at tray and plate respectively
- knife tool held by robot or at some location
- bowl at tray
- location names: tray, plate, elevated_platform (or kitchen_counter), chopping_board

The robot should start not holding anything, but objects at their locations, and the knife at a known location. The robot can then pick the knife and carrot, slice carrot, place carrot in bowl, pick cucumber, place cucumber sliced (assuming it is already sliced from image) in bowl.

Since cucumber is already sliced (shown cut), we can treat it as sliced initially or alternatively, since only carrot is instructed to slice, cucumber can just be placed in bowl.

Here is the corrected PDDL problem:

```lisp
(define (problem slice-veg)
  (:domain cooking)
  (:objects
    robot1 - robot
    carrot cucumber bowl knife chopping_board - object
    carrot_veg cucumber_veg - vegetable
    tool1 - tool
    tray plate elevated_platform - location
  )
  (:init
    (at robot1 tray)
    (at carrot tray)
    (at carrot_veg tray)
    (at cucumber plate)
    (at cucumber_veg plate)
    (at bowl tray)
    (at knife elevated_platform)
    (at chopping_board elevated_platform)
  )
  (:goal
    (and
      (sliced carrot_veg)
      (placed_in carrot_veg bowl)
      (placed_in cucumber_veg bowl)
    )
  )
)
```
This configuration allows the robot to pick knife and carrot, slice carrot, place carrot and cucumber into bowl.
