The error in the previous PDDL problem was that the robot was carrying the knife initially, but there was no action that allowed the robot to place the knife down on the cutting_board. The vegetable "tom" was at the cutting_board, but the knife needed to be at the cutting_board too for slicing to be possible according to the preconditions of the slice action. The knife can't be both "available" and "carried" by the robot simultaneously — it must be placed at the cutting_board first to enable slicing there.

Also, the carrot was already at the bowl, so no action was necessary for placing it there, which is okay.

To correct this, I will place the knife at the cutting_board (available), and the robot is free initially. This way, the robot can pick the knife, then slice the tomato on the cutting_board, and finally place the sliced tomato and carrot to the bowl.

Here is the corrected PDDL problem:

```lisp
(define (problem cooking1)
  (:domain cooking)
  (:objects
    tom - vegetable
    carrot - vegetable
    bowl - location
    cutting_board - location
    robot1 - robot
    knife - tool
  )
  (:init
    (is-whole tom)
    (is-whole carrot)
    (available tom)
    (available carrot)
    (available knife)
    (free robot1)
    (can-cut knife)
    (at tom cutting_board)
    (at carrot bowl)
    (at knife cutting_board)
    (is-workspace cutting_board)
  )
  (:goal
    (and
      (is-sliced tom)
      (not (is-whole tom))
      (at tom bowl)
      (at carrot bowl)
    )
  )
)
```
