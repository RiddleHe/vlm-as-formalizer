The error in the previous PDDL problem is that the knife tool is initially located "at robot1" rather than at a specific location. According to the domain predicates, the knife must be "available" and "at" some specific location to be picked up by the robot before slicing can occur. Also, the knife was not explicitly marked as "available" in the previous problem.

In the image, the knife is positioned above the cutting board, so it should be located at the cutting_board location initially rather than with the robot. The robots are free and no tool is held currently. The carrot and tomato are whole and at the counter, which matches the image setting. The bowl is also on the counter.

A corrected formulation considers the knife at the cutting_board location and available to be picked. The robot must first pick the knife from the cutting_board, then slice the vegetables on the workspace cutting_board. The goal remains to have the carrot and tomato sliced and placed in the bowl.

Here is the corrected PDDL problem:

```lisp
(define (problem cooking_task2)
(:domain cooking)
(:objects
    robot1 robot2 - robot
    carrot tomato - vegetable
    knife - tool
    cutting_board bowl counter - location
)
(:init
    (free robot1)
    (free robot2)

    (available carrot)
    (available tomato)
    (available knife)

    (is-whole carrot)
    (is-whole tomato)

    (can-cut knife)

    (at carrot counter)
    (at tomato counter)
    (at knife cutting_board)
    (at bowl counter)
    (is-workspace cutting_board)
)
(:goal
    (and
        (is-sliced carrot)
        (is-sliced tomato)
        (at carrot bowl)
        (at tomato bowl)
    )
)
)
```
