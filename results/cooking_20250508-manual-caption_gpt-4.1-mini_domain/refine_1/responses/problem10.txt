The error in the previous PDDL problem is that the tomato, which needs to be sliced, is on the counter, and according to the domain constraints, slicing requires the vegetable to be on the cutting board. However, the tomato was not placed on the cutting board initially, and there is no action in the domain to move it there except if a robot picks and places it. But since the tomato was on the counter and the robot holding the knife did not pick the tomato before slicing, the plan is not achievable.

To fix this:
- The tomato must be moved to the cutting board before slicing.
- The robot holding the knife can slice only when the vegetable is on the cutting board.
- The robots and objects should be consistent with the image, too.

Observation from the image:
- There are two robots.
- One robot holds a knife.
- There is a bowl on the left.
- The carrots are on a round wooden cutting board.
- The tomato is in front of the bowl, on the table (not on the cutting board).
- The cutting board is the wooden board where the carrots are placed.

Thus, to make the tomato sliced, it should be placed on the cutting board by the free robot before slicing.

Here is the corrected PDDL problem:

```lisp
(define (problem cooking_task2)
(:domain cooking)
(:objects
    robot1
    robot2
    bowl1 - object
    tomato1 - vegetable
    carrot1 - vegetable
    carrot2 - vegetable
    knife1 - tool
    counter1 - location
    cutting_board1 - location
)
(:init
    (counter counter1)
    (cutting_board cutting_board1)
    (bowl bowl1)
    (vegetable tomato1)
    (vegetable carrot1)
    (vegetable carrot2)
    (knife knife1)
    (on bowl1 counter1)
    (on tomato1 counter1)
    (on carrot1 cutting_board1)
    (on carrot2 cutting_board1)
    (free robot1)
    (holding robot2 knife1)
)
(:goal (and
    (sliced tomato1)
    (on tomato1 bowl1)
    (on carrot1 bowl1)
    (on carrot2 bowl1)
))
)
```
