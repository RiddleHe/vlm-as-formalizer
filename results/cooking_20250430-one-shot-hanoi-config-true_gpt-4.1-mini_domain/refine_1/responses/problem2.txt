The error in the previous PDDL problem was that the tomato was never picked up, so it could not be placed in the bowl. Also, the tomato was intended to be placed "in" the bowl but there was no action defined to put the tomato in the bowl after slicing. The slice action only makes the tomato sliced but does not put it in the bowl, and there was no plan to hold or place the tomato in the bowl after slicing.

To fix this, the PDDL problem should ensure:
- The robot that holds the knife slices the tomato.
- The tomato is picked up after slicing.
- The tomato is placed into the bowl location (the bowl acts as a location here).
- The goal must be achievable with the current actions (pick, place, slice).

We add the tomato as an object to be held after slicing, then place it on the bowl location.

Here is the corrected PDDL problem:

```lisp
(define (problem cooking_task_corrected)
(:domain cooking)
(:objects
    robot1
    robot2
    knife1 - tool
    tomato1 - vegetable
    bowl1 - bowl
    loc1 loc2 loc3 - location
)
(:init
    (holding robot1 knife1)
    (knife knife1)
    (tomato tomato1)
    (at tomato1 loc2)
    (bowl_obj bowl1)
    (at bowl1 loc1)
    (at robot1 loc3)
    (at robot2 loc3)
)
(:goal (and
    (sliced tomato1)
    (at tomato1 loc1)
))
)
```
