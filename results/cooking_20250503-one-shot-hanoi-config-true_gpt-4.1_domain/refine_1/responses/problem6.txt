The error in the previous PDDL problem was that the goals required `(in-bowl cucumber bowl1)` and `(in-bowl carrot bowl1)`, but only the **sliced** vegetables should go into the bowl, based on the instruction. Furthermore, the predicates `in-bowl` operate on **object**, while the domain has separate types for `vegetable` and `sliced-vegetable`, and the only way to put sliced objects in the bowl is to reference the new objects produced by slicing. Also, the `is-bowl` and `is-vegetable` predicates are not specified in the domain, so they should not be in the `:init` section. The previous state also wrongly placed both vegetables and the bowl on `tray1`, and didn't correctly transition food to the board before slicing.

Here is the corrected PDDL problem:

```
(define (problem cooking-slice-and-bowl)
(:domain cooking)
(:objects
    robot1 robot2 - robot
    cucumber carrot - vegetable
    sliced-cucumber sliced-carrot - sliced-vegetable
    bowl1 - bowl
    knife1 - tool
    board1 - location
    tray1 - location
)
(:init
    (at cucumber tray1)
    (at carrot tray1)
    (at bowl1 tray1)
    (at knife1 board1)
    (on-board board1)
    (is-knife knife1)
    (handfree robot1)
    (holding robot2 knife1)
)
(:goal (and
    (is-sliced cucumber)
    (is-sliced carrot)
    (exists-sliced sliced-cucumber)
    (exists-sliced sliced-carrot)
    (in-bowl sliced-cucumber bowl1)
    (in-bowl sliced-carrot bowl1)
))
)
```
