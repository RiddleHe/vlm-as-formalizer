The error in the previous PDDL problem is that both the cucumber and the tomato are located on the "table," but the `slice` action requires vegetables to be "on-chopping-board." The predicates `(on-chopping-board cucumber)` and `(on-chopping-board tomato)` were present, which is good, but their locations were not the chopping board in the `(at ... )` predicates; they were set to the table. 

For the plan to work, the locations of cucumber and tomato in the `(at ...)` predicates must be the "chopping_board" rather than the "table" to satisfy the `slice` action precondition: `(at ?veg ?loc)` where `?loc` is the location corresponding to the chopping board.

Additionally, the knife is held by one robot and is at the table, which is fine as the starting state.

Here is the corrected PDDL problem:

```
(define (problem slice_and_bowl)
(:domain cooking)
(:objects
    robot1 robot2 - robot
    cucumber tomato - vegetable
    knife - tool
    bowl1 - bowl
    chopping_board table - location
)
(:init
    (handfree robot1)
    (holding robot2 knife)
    (at cucumber chopping_board)
    (at tomato chopping_board)
    (at knife table)
    (at bowl1 table)
    (on-chopping-board cucumber)
    (on-chopping-board tomato)
)
(:goal (and
    (sliced cucumber)
    (sliced tomato)
    (in-bowl cucumber bowl1)
    (in-bowl tomato bowl1)
))
)
```
