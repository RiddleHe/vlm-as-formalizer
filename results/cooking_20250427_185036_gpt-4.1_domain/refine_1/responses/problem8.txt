The main error in the previous problem definition is with the representation of "sliced cucumber" and the tomato. According to the domain, after slicing, the cucumber becomes a new object of type "sliced-object" (not the original cucumber1). Placing unsliced vegetables (e.g., tomato1) in the bowl does not match the instruction, as tomatoes are not required to be sliced—they should be placed in the bowl as they are. However, only the sliced cucumber slices (represented by a new object) can be placed in the bowl after slicing. Also, it is necessary to use "(is-sliced-object ...)" and "(slices-of ... ...)" for sliced cucumber. The original PDDL attempted to place both unsliced tomatoes and the cucumber (which is meant to be sliced) in the bowl, but the domain rules mean cucumber1 will not exist after slicing, only its slices will.

- Introduce a new object for sliced cucumber.
- Use (slices-of cucumber1_slices cucumber1).
- Ensure that the goal is placing the sliced cucumber (cucumber1_slices) and the whole tomatoes in the bowl.
