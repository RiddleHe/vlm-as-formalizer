The error message "Predicate not defined in domain: not" indicates that the PDDL parser does not accept the usage of the "not" keyword as a predicate by itself. The "not" construct is used for negation in preconditions and effects, but you cannot use it as a predicate in the init or goal sections.

In the last response, the problem was using:

```
(not (is-sliced carrot))
(not (is-sliced cucumber))
```

in the `(:init ...)` section, which is invalid syntax. Instead, you simply do not mention the predicate if it is false—negated predicates should only be used inside the `:precondition` or `:effect` of actions, or inside a properly constructed negation.

Also for initialization of a predicate being false, do not write `(not (is-sliced carrot))` in init, just omit `(is-sliced carrot)` altogether, since predicates are by default false unless specified otherwise.
