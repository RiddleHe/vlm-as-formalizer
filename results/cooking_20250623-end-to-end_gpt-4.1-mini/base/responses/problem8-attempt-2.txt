The error in the PDDL file is a semantic PDDL error. Specifically, the use of "(not (is-sliced cucumber))" and "(not (is-whole cucumber))" directly inside the goal is invalid because PDDL goals cannot include negated predicates directly like this. Instead, to specify that a vegetable is sliced and not whole, only the positive predicate (is-sliced cucumber) should be required in the goal. The negative condition "(not (is-whole cucumber))" is implied by the domain effects of the slice action and does not need to be stated explicitly in the goal.

Also, there is a semantic misunderstanding in using two tomato objects tomato1 and tomato2, when from the image there are two tomato halves already sliced and placed on the cutting board, so they should be considered already sliced. Given the instruction to place sliced tomatoes in the bowl, the goal should specify the sliced tomatoes available at the bowl, but they should be declared as already sliced from the start.

Additionally, the "available" predicate is provided for indicating pickable objects, but for the tomatoes already sliced and on the board, the predicate should reflect their sliced state.

Hence, the corrections needed include:
- Remove negated predicates in the goal.
- Declare the two tomato halves as already sliced from the start.
- Reflect the initial state with one robot free, one carrying the knife.
- Include the cucumber whole and available for slicing.
- Locations and objects should match exactly the scene: robot1, robot2; knife; cucumber; tomato1 and tomato2 halves; bowl; cutting_board; workspace.

(:domain cooking)
(:objects
