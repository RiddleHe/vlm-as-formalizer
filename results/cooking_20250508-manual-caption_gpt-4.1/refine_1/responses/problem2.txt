Analysis of Error:
---
The error occurs because according to the domain definition, for slicing to occur, the vegetable (here, tomato) must be at a location that is a workspace (`is-workspace ?loc`), and specifically, the cutting board is the only logical workspace for slicing. After the slicing action, the tomato becomes sliced and must then be placed into the bowl.

However, in the last generated PDDL:
- The tomato starts at `counter`, not `cutting_board`, so it cannot be sliced directly.
- There is no plan in which the robot can place/slice the tomato without first picking it up from the counter and placing it onto the cutting board.
- Moreover, the bowl is modeled as an object and as a location; the correct modeling is that ingredients should go into containers placed at a workspace/location, so placing a tomato "in" a bowl may need to be represented as `at tomato bowl` if a bowl is treated as a location, or by putting the bowl at a workspace and then placing the tomato into it.

Additionally:
- After slicing, the tomato must be placed in the bowl.
- The availability of the tomato after slicing should be ensured to allow the robot to pick it up and put it in the bowl.
- The correct sequence must allow: robot picks tomato from counter, places it on cutting board, slices it (while holding a knife), then picks sliced tomato from cutting board and places it in the bowl.

Revised and Corrected PDDL Problem
---

(define (problem cooking-slice-tomato)
(:domain cooking)
(:objects
    robot1 robot2 - robot
    tomato - vegetable
    knife - tool
    bowl - object
    counter cutting_board - location
)
(:init
    (is-whole tomato)
    (not (is-sliced tomato))
    (at tomato counter)
    (at bowl counter)
    (carry robot2 knife)
    (can-cut knife)
    (free robot1)
    (not (free robot2))
    (is-workspace cutting_board)
    (is-workspace counter)
    (available tomato)
    (available bowl)
)
(:goal (and
    (is-sliced tomato)
    (at tomato bowl)
))
)
