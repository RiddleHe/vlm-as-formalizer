```pddl
(define (problem block_swapping)
    (:domain blocksworld)
    (:objects 
        green_block orange_block red_block purple_block blue_block yellow_block - block
        robot1 - robot
    )
    (:init
        (on green_block yellow_block)
        (on yellow_block orange_block)
        (clear green_block)
        (handempty robot1)
        (ontable blue_block)
        (clear blue_block)
        (clear red_block)
        (clear purple_block)
        (ontable red_block)
        (ontable purple_block)
    )
    (:goal 
        (and 
            (on blue_block yellow_block)
            (on orange_block red_block)
            (on red_block purple_block)
            (on green_block blue_block)
        )
    )
)
```

### Explanation:
- **Objects**: Define blocks (green_block, orange_block, red_block, purple_block, blue_block, yellow_block) and a robot (robot1).
- **Initial State**:
  - The blocks on the right stack are initially stacked such that the green block is on top, and the yellow and orange blocks form the base.
  - The blue block is on the table and is clear.
  - Other blocks (red and purple blocks) are on the table and are clear.
  - Robot `robot1` is empty-handed.
- **Goal**:
  - The goal state should have:
    - The blue block on top of the yellow block.
    - The green block stacks on the blue block.
    - The orange block on the red block.
    - The red block on the purple block.

These conditions meet the instruction to create the specified stacks in the workspace.
