Let's analyze the task carefully:

**Initial state:**

- One robot (robot1), empty-handed.

- Four stacks:

  1. Stack 1: yellow  
  2. Stack 2: red  
  3. Stack 3: orange over blue over green (top to bottom: orange on blue, blue on green, green on table)  
  4. Stack 4: purple  

So the initial "on" relationships:

- on orange blue  
- on blue green  
- ontable green  
- ontable yellow  
- ontable red  
- ontable purple  

All blocks except the bottom ones are not ontable.

Clear blocks initially are the top ones:

- orange (top of stack 3)  
- yellow (top of stack 1)  
- red (top of stack 2)  
- purple (top of stack 4)  

**Goal:**

Two stacks:

- blue over orange  
- yellow over green over red over purple  

Translating stacks bottom to top:

Stack A: blue ontable, orange on blue (orange top)  
Stack B: yellow on green, green on red, red on purple, purple ontable (yellow top)  

Note: Because "ontable" can only be for bottom blocks as per domain, so blue and purple are on the table in goal.

Goal predicates will be the on and ontable relations as above.

---

**Objects**:

Blocks: yellow, red, orange, blue, green, purple  
Robot: robot1

---

**Initial state predicates:**

- ontables: green, yellow, red, purple  
- on orange blue  
- on blue green  
- clear orange  
- clear yellow  
- clear red  
- clear purple  
- handempty robot1  

---

**Goal predicates:**

- on orange blue  
- on blue (ontable)  
- yellow on green  
- green on red  
- red on purple  
- purple (ontable)  

---

Here is the full PDDL problem:

```lisp
(define (problem blocks_problem)
  (:domain blocksworld)
  (:objects
    yellow red orange blue green purple - block
    robot1 - robot
  )
  (:init
    (ontable green)
    (ontable yellow)
    (ontable red)
    (ontable purple)
    (on orange blue)
    (on blue green)
    (clear orange)
    (clear yellow)
    (clear red)
    (clear purple)
    (handempty robot1)
  )
  (:goal (and
    (on orange blue)
    (ontable blue)
    (on yellow green)
    (on green red)
    (on red purple)
    (ontable purple)
  ))
)
```
This problem reflects the initial arrangement and targets the desired stacks in the goal.
